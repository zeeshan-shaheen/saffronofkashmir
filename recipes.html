Six Ways to Cook with Mongra Saffron

From the mountains of Kashmir to your kitchen in the UAE — each recipe uses Mongra grade saffron for maximum colour, aroma, and flavour. Always bloom saffron in warm liquid before cooking.

All Recipes

15 min Kashmiri

Kashmiri Kahwa

The jewel of Kashmiri hospitality — a fragrant green tea scented with saffron, cardamom, and cinnamon, served at every celebration and offered to every guest.

Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 8 Mongra saffron threads
  • 1 tbsp green tea leaves (or 1 green tea bag)
  • 4 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 tsp raw honey or sugar
  • 8–10 almonds, blanched and slivered
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)
  • 2 cups (500ml) water

Method

  1. Bring water to a boil in a small saucepan. Add cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. Reduce to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the green tea leaves and saffron threads. Remove from heat and steep for 3 minutes — no longer, or the tea becomes bitter.
  3. Strain into small cups through a fine-mesh strainer. Add honey or sugar to taste.
  4. Garnish with slivered almonds and a few drops of rose water. Serve immediately.
Saffron Tip: For a deeper golden colour and richer flavour, bloom your saffron threads in 1 tablespoon of warm water for 10 minutes before adding to the brew. Never add saffron to boiling water — heat above 85°C degrades the aromatic safranal compound.
50 min Kashmiri

Zafrani Pulao

Fragrant Kashmiri saffron rice with caramelised onions, whole spices, and a deep golden hue — the ceremonial rice of Kashmir, served at weddings and feasts.

Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups aged basmati rice, soaked 30 minutes
  • 12–15 Mongra saffron threads
  • 3 tbsp warm whole milk (for blooming)
  • 4 tbsp pure ghee
  • 2 medium onions, finely sliced
  • 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 4 cardamoms, 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp fennel powder
  • ½ tsp dried ginger powder (sonth)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3½ cups water
  • 2 tbsp fried cashews and raisins to garnish

Method

  1. Bloom saffron in warm milk for 20 minutes. The milk should turn a rich golden colour.
  2. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices and fry for 1 minute until fragrant. Add sliced onions and fry on medium heat until deep golden-brown, about 15 minutes.
  3. Drain soaked rice and add to the pot. Stir gently to coat in the ghee. Add fennel powder, sonth, and salt.
  4. Pour in 3½ cups water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cover and cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Drizzle the saffron milk over the surface of the rice without stirring. Re-cover and steam on the lowest heat for 12 more minutes.
  6. Remove from heat. Rest 5 minutes. Gently fluff with a fork and garnish with fried cashews and raisins.
Saffron Tip: Use only warm (not hot) milk for blooming. The ideal temperature is 60°C — test it on your wrist, it should feel warm but not scalding. This preserves the safranal aromatics while fully extracting the crocin pigment.
10 min Kashmiri

Kesar Doodh

Warm saffron milk — the traditional Kashmiri wellness drink. Served before sleep for over a thousand years for its calming, anti-inflammatory properties.

Serves 1

Ingredients

  • 250ml full-fat whole milk
  • 8–10 Mongra saffron threads
  • 1 tbsp warm milk (for pre-blooming)
  • 1 tsp raw honey or sugar
  • 3 green cardamom pods, seeds only, crushed
  • A pinch of turmeric (optional, for the golden milk variation)
  • Pistachios and a few saffron threads for garnish

Method

  1. In a small bowl, bloom the saffron threads in 1 tablespoon of warm milk for 10 minutes. The saffron will release a deep amber colour.
  2. Warm the remaining milk in a saucepan over medium heat. Do not boil — heat just until steaming, around 70°C.
  3. Crush cardamom seeds lightly and add to the milk. Stir for 1 minute, then strain or leave uncrushed based on preference.
  4. Pour the warm milk into your cup. Add the saffron bloom (liquid and threads), honey, and stir gently.
  5. Garnish with a few slivered pistachios and 2–3 additional saffron threads. Drink warm before bed.
Wellness Tip: Research suggests consuming 30mg of saffron daily (approximately 8–10 Mongra threads) provides antioxidant and mild antidepressant effects. Kesar Doodh is the simplest daily vehicle for this dose.
90 min Gulf Arabic

Arabic Saffron Machboos

The Gulf's most celebrated rice dish — slow-cooked spiced chicken over fragrant long-grain rice, finished with saffron and rose water. A UAE table centrepiece.

Serves 4–6

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (or 1kg thighs)
  • 2 cups long-grain or basmati rice, soaked
  • 15 Mongra saffron threads
  • 4 tbsp warm water (for saffron bloom)
  • 2 tbsp rose water
  • 3 onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • 2 tsp baharat (Arabic 7-spice blend)
  • 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp cumin, ½ tsp black lime powder (loomi)
  • Salt, dried black lime (loomi) whole
  • 3 cups chicken stock
  • Fresh coriander and fried onions to serve

Method

  1. Bloom saffron in 4 tbsp warm water for 20 minutes. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large heavy pot. Fry onions until golden-brown (15–20 minutes). Add garlic and fry 2 more minutes.
  3. Add chicken pieces. Brown on all sides, about 8 minutes. Add baharat, turmeric, cumin, and loomi powder. Stir to coat.
  4. Add tomatoes and dried loomi. Cook until tomatoes break down, 10 minutes. Pour in chicken stock, bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 30 minutes.
  5. Remove chicken. Strain the cooking broth — you need 3 cups. Add drained rice to the pot with the broth. Bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 15 minutes.
  6. Place chicken pieces on top of the partially cooked rice. Drizzle with the saffron water and rose water. Cover with a tight lid and steam on very low heat for 15 more minutes.
  7. Serve on a large platter with the chicken on top. Garnish with fresh coriander and crispy fried onions.
Saffron Tip: Add the saffron bloom and rose water together in the final steaming stage — this preserves the floral, aromatic top notes that would be lost if saffron is added to the broth during the long simmer.
25 min + 4hr chill Italian-Kashmiri

Saffron Panna Cotta

A silky Italian custard elevated with Kashmiri saffron — ivory and gold in a glass, tasting of cream, honey, and the faintest whisper of the crocus flower.

Makes 4 portions

Ingredients

  • 500ml (2 cups) heavy cream
  • 12 Mongra saffron threads
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 2½ tsp powdered gelatin (or 2.5 gelatin leaves)
  • 3 tbsp cold water
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • To serve: honey, pistachio crumble, 2–3 saffron threads per glass

Method

  1. Sprinkle gelatin over cold water in a small bowl. Leave to bloom for 5 minutes.
  2. In a saucepan, heat cream with saffron threads and sugar over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves. Do not boil. Remove from heat when steaming.
  3. Stir in vanilla. Add the bloomed gelatin and whisk until completely dissolved (return to very low heat for 30 seconds if needed).
  4. Strain through a fine sieve into a pouring jug. Pour into serving glasses or ramekins. Allow to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  5. To serve: drizzle with raw honey, scatter pistachio crumble, and place 2–3 Mongra saffron threads on top for visual drama.
Chef's Note: The saffron infuses the cream as it heats — don't strain it out until step 4. This gives the panna cotta its distinctive golden hue and full saffron flavour without any graininess.
10 min Modern Kashmiri

Saffron Lemonade

A refreshing summer drink that's become a UAE party staple — the tartness of fresh lemon balanced by the sweet, floral richness of Mongra saffron syrup.

Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 10 Mongra saffron threads
  • 3 tbsp warm water (for blooming)
  • ½ cup (120ml) fresh lemon juice (4–5 lemons)
  • ⅓ cup (80ml) simple syrup (1:1 sugar and water)
  • 3 cups cold water or sparkling water
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon slices and 2–3 saffron threads per glass to garnish
  • Fresh mint (optional)

Method

  1. Bloom saffron in 3 tablespoons of warm (not hot) water for 10 minutes. The water will turn a rich amber-gold.
  2. In a large pitcher, combine lemon juice, simple syrup, and the saffron bloom (liquid and threads). Stir well.
  3. Add cold water (or sparkling water for a fizzy version). Taste and adjust sweetness.
  4. Fill glasses with ice. Pour lemonade over. Garnish with a lemon slice, fresh mint, and 2–3 Mongra saffron threads floating on top.
  5. Serve immediately. The saffron bloom will continue colouring the drink for 5–10 minutes — the longer it sits, the deeper the golden hue.
For a UAE twist: Replace the simple syrup with 3 tablespoons of our Saffron Honey — available in our product range. The floral complexity of honey saffron syrup makes this lemonade extraordinary for Iftar and Eid gatherings.

The Golden Rule

Always Bloom Your Saffron First

Whatever recipe you're making — always bloom your Mongra saffron threads in warm (not boiling) liquid for 10–20 minutes before adding to your dish. This releases the crocin pigment and safranal aromatic compound, giving you maximum colour, flavour, and health benefit from every thread.
