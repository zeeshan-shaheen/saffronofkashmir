The Chinar — Kashmir's Sacred Tree

Our Heritage

"Only the finest stigma tips — the crimson crown of the crocus — earn the name Mongra."

Deep in the Karewa plateau of Pampore — a crescent of fertile land just south of Srinagar — the saffron crocus has bloomed every October for over 700 years. The town of Pampore holds 90% of India's saffron production, and its terroir is irreplaceable: the clay-rich, well-drained soil, the sharp autumn nights, and the dawn mists that roll in from Dal Lake all conspire to produce a saffron of extraordinary colour, aroma, and medicinal potency.

Mongra is the highest grade of Kashmiri saffron — only the deep-red stigma tips, separated from the yellow style. Just 3 stigmas per flower. Roughly 150,000 flowers for one kilogram. Saffron of Kashmir has been sourcing Mongra grade exclusively since our founding in 2004, working directly with farming families who have tended these fields across generations.
